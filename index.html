What Defines a Growth Stock?

Growth stocks are shares in companies expected to grow significantly faster than the average company. Their value is based on future potential, not current profits. They reinvest earnings to scale rapidly, aiming for market leadership and innovation.

Impact of Economic Policy

Growth stock valuations are highly sensitive to interest rates. Generally, as interest rates rise, the present value of future earnings decreases, putting downward pressure on stock prices.

The Investor's Takeaway

1

Think Long-Term

Growth investing requires patience. The biggest gains are often realized over years, not months.

2

Do Your Homework

Thoroughly research a company's competitive advantage and business model before investing.

3

Diversify Your Portfolio

Don't put all your eggs in one growth basket. Diversification helps manage inherent volatility.
